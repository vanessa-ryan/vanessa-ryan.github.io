Banana Bread

Ingredients:

3 Ripe Bananas

70g Melted Butter

140g Sugar

2 Eggs

1 tsp Vanilla Extract

1 tsp Cinamon

1 tsp Baking Powder

200g Flour

Chocolate chips

Cooking time : 30 minutes at 180 degrees

Method:

Mash the bananas in a mixing bowl, add melted butter, sugar, eggs and vanilla extract. Whisk well until combined.

Now mix in the dry ingredients by adding flour. Add baking powder and cinamon. Lastly, mix in the chocolate chips. The end result will be athick mixture.

Pre heat the oven to 180 degrees. Line a bread tin in parchment paper and put in mixture. Bake at 180 for 30 minutes and leave to cool for 15 minutes.

"You can never have enough chocolate chips" - Ness, 2024

For added flavour, feel free to add walnuts, raisins or cocoa powder.

Banana bread is super easy to make and perfect for cozy nights in.

Chocolate Chunk Cookies

Ingredients:

120g Butter

150g Sugar

1 Egg

1 tsp Vanilla Extract

180g Plain Flour

1 tsp Bicarbonate of Soda

Dark Chocolate chips

Cooking time : 12 minutes at 180 degrees

Method:

Add Butter and sugar together. Mix until light and creamy, then beat in the egg and vanilla. Once combined, stir in the plain flour, bicarbonate soda, chocolate and 1tsp of salt (optional).

Pre heat the oven to 180 degrees. Line 2 trays with parchment baking paper and put in mixture. Scoop mixture into small circles on the tray, leaving enoug hspace between each to allow for spreading. There should be 6 per tray.

Bake at 180 for 12 minutes and leave to cool for 10 minutes.

"I like to have them warm and gooey so I undercook them by a few minutes" - Ness, 2024

To make the cookie shape extra thick and chunky, leave batter in fridge for 30 minutes before cooking.

You can add added ingredients of your choice. I personally like biscoff or white chocolate in the cookie.

Apple Crumble

Ingredients:

3 Apples

110g Butter

110g Sugar

175g Plain Flour

1 tbsp Rolled Oats

Cooking time : 35-40 minutes at 180 degrees

Method:

Peel and slice apples into small chunks and mix with 2 tbsp sugar. Place in baking dish and flatten.

In a bowl, mix flour and sugar, then add cold butter and rub it in with your fingers until it is the consistency of breadcrumbs.

Pour the crumb mix over the apples in the bakign dish and spread evenly. Sprinkle sugar over the top for an extra crunch.

Bake in over for 35-40 minutes until it turns a golden colour and the apples are soft inside. Leave to cool for 10 minutes.

"Best served with custard" - Ness, 2024

Serve with ice cream or custard. You can use all sorts of fruits e.g. blackberry, strawberry, rhubarb.
